Search Interface Design Study

Thank you for agreeing to participate in this study related on Search Interface Design.

Important Notes

  • Please do not use the "Previous Page" or "Next Page" functions of your web browser to navigate yourself through the process.
  • At the end of the experiment you will be redirected back to MTurk to submit the HIT.
  • In order to conduct this experiment you need to use Safari, Firefox or Chrome. If you use any other browser, your HIT may not be accepted.
  • Your screen resolution should be at least 1024x720 or above, otherwise your HIT will not be accepted.
  • Please read instructions throughout this experiment carefully. You will not be able to return to previous pages or resubmit your answers.

Your Role

  • For this study, imagine that you are a newspaper reporter, where you will need to write a story on four topics. In order to accomplish this you will need to search for related information.

The Search System

  • The search system that you will use connects to a database of about 1 million newspaper articles from the New York Times, The Associated Press and Xinhua news agencies. The articles within the database were published in 1998 to 2000. The search system you will be using looks similar to a web search engine, though there might be some slight differences to the search interface.

The Search Tasks

  • For each search task, you'll be given a short description of a topic and what information would be relevant to this topic.
  • This can be viewed by the Show Task button at the top right-hand corner of the screen.
  • You need to pose queries and examine results, just like you would in a web search engine.
  • When viewing documents, you will be able to select the Mark as Relevant button when you find a document relevant to the topic.
  • There are over 100 relevant documents in the database for each topic.

Goal and Incentive

  • Your goal is to find as many relevant documents as possible during the time allocated (approximately 10 minutes per task).
  • As an additional incentive if you identified many relevant documents with high accuracy you will earn more.
  • From previous studies, we have a GOLD standard of relevant documents, and the past performance of other searchers:
    • If you are in the top 50% of searchers, you will earn an extra $1
    • If you are in the top 25% of searchers, you will earn an extra $2.50
    • To do so, you need to identify many relevant documents, but also with high accuracy i.e. the documents that you mark as relevant are also relevant with respect to our GOLD standard.
  • The experiment will take approximately 50 minutes of your time.

Experiment Outline

Before you start searching, we will ask you a few questions about you.

We will then give you a few minutes to get comfortable with the search interface by undertaking a practice task.

You'll then move onto the four main search tasks. Before each task, you will complete a pre-task questionnaire about your knowledge of the search topic. After you complete the task, you will complete a post-task questionnaire about the search interface.

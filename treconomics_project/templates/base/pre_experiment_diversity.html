News Search System Study

Thank you for agreeing to participate in this study related on News Search Systems.

Important Notes

  • Please do not use the "Back" or "Forward" functionality of your Web browser to navigate yourself through the process.
  • At the end of the experiment, you will be redirected back to MTurk to submit the HIT.
  • In order to conduct this experiment you need to use Safari, Firefox or Chrome. If you use any other browser, your HIT may not be accepted.
  • Your screen resolution should be at least 1024x768 or above, otherwise your HIT will not be accepted.
  • Please read instructions throughout this experiment carefully. You will not be able to return to previous pages or resubmit your answers.

Your Role

  • For this study, imagine that you are a newspaper reporter, where you will need to write a story on four topics. In order to accomplish this you will need to search for related background information.

The Search Systems

  • The search systems that you will use connect to a database of about 1 million news articles from the New York Times, The Associated Press and Xinhua news agencies. The articles within the database were published in 1998 to 2000. The search system you will be using looks similar to a web search engine, though there might be some slight differences to the search interface.

The Search Tasks

  • For each search task, you'll be given a short description of a topic and what information would be relevant to this topic.
  • This can be viewed by the Show Task button at the top right-hand corner of the screen.
  • You need to pose queries and examine results, just like you would in a web search engine.
  • When viewing documents, you will be able to select the Mark as Relevant button when you find a document relevant to the topic.
  • There are over 100 relevant documents in the database for each topic.

Goal

  • Your goal is to find and SAVE {{ target }} useful documents for each tasks.
  • From previous studies, we have a GOLD standard of useful documents.
  • Pay close attention to the task description - as there are two kinds of search task.
    • (1) Find documents that match the criteria specified i.e. RELEVANT
    • (2) Find documents that match the criteria specified but report different aspects, i.e. RELEVANT and DIFFERENT.
    • For example, let's say the topic is on "People Smuggling" and the criteria states that a RELEVANT document must identify the country where people were smuggled from, how they were smuggled and how many.
    • For search tasks where we ask for RELEVANT documents, if you find 4 documents where people from Canada have been smuggled into the US, these are all considered useful.
    • However, for search task were we ask for RELEVANT and DIFFERENT documents, if you find 4 documents where people from Canada have been smuggled into the US. Only 1 document is considered useful. Therefore, other documents need to be found, i.e. involving smuggling from Mexico, Chile, etc. to count as being useful, (RELEVANT and DIFFERENT).
    • Note: You can review your saved documents and the task description while searching.

Success and Payment

  • You will be paid ONLY IF you successfully complete all four tasks.
  • To be successful you must have saved at least {{ target }} useful documents per search task, AND
  • No more than {{ target }} documents that are not useful per search task, i.e. accuracy greater than 50 percent.
  • That is, if you save more NOT RELEVANT documents than RELEVANT documents, the task will be deemed unsuccessful.
  • From previous studies, most participants identified 5-10 RELEVANT documents in around 7-8 minutes with accuracy greater than 50 percent.
  • Thus the experiment will take approximately 40-50 minutes of your time.

Experiment Outline

Before you start searching, we will ask a few questions about you.

We will then give you a few minutes to get comfortable with the search interface by undertaking a practice task.

You'll then move onto the four main search tasks. Before each task, you will complete a pre-task questionnaire about your knowledge of the search topic. After you complete the task, you will complete a post-task questionnaire about the search interface.
